# Malaria

## Causative Agent (Plasmodium Species)
- *P. falciparum*
  - Most severe ==Severe Malaria==
  - Malignant tertian fever
  - Infects all RBCs
  - Morphology: Delicate ring, ==applique/accolé==, double dots. Trophozoite: Compact. Schizont: Rare in peripheral blood. ==Gametocyte: Crescent/Banana==.
  - Complications: ==Cerebral Malaria==, ==Blackwater fever==, Severe Anemia, ARDS, AKI, Hypoglycemia.
  - Chloroquine resistant.
- *P. vivax*
  - Benign tertian fever
  - Infects Reticulocytes (enlarged RBCs)
  - Morphology: Delicate ring. Trophozoite: ==Amoeboid==. Schizont: 12-24 merozoites. Gametocyte: Round/Oval.
  - ==Schüffner's dots== (punctation) in RBCs.
  - Forms ==Hypnozoites== in liver (relapse).
- *P. ovale*
  - Benign tertian fever
  - Infects Reticulocytes (enlarged, ==fimbriated/oval== RBCs)
  - Morphology: Similar to vivax, RBCs often oval with fimbriated edges.
  - Forms ==Hypnozoites== in liver (relapse).
  - Geographic: Primarily West Africa.
- *P. malariae*
  - ==Quartan fever== (72 hr cycle)
  - Infects mature RBCs
  - Morphology: Compact ring. Trophozoite: ==Band form==. Schizont: 6-12 merozoites, ==Rosette form==.
  - No Hypnozoites --> ==Recrudescence== (years later possible).
- *P. knowlesi*
  - ==Quotidian fever== (24 hr cycle)
  - Zoonotic (Simian malaria)
  - Can cause severe disease, rapid progression.
  - Morphology similar to Falciparum/Malariae but faster cycle. PCR for confirmation.

## Life Cycle (Human - Asexual)
- **Infection:** ==Sporozoites== from mosquito saliva infect Liver.
- **Liver Stage (Exoerythrocytic):** Sporozoites develop into Schizonts --> Merozoites.
  - ==Hypnozoites== (dormant forms) in *P. vivax* & *P. ovale*.
- **Blood Stage (Erythrocytic):** Merozoites infect RBCs --> Ring --> Trophozoite --> Schizont --> Merozoites --> RBC rupture.
  - Symptoms occur here.
- **Gametocyte Stage:** Some merozoites develop into male/female Gametocytes --> ingested by mosquito.

## Clinical Features
- **Uncomplicated:** Fever, Chills, Headache, Myalgia, GI symptoms, Splenomegaly.
- **==Severe Malaria Criteria== (Any one of these):**
  - Prostration
  - Impaired consciousness / ==Cerebral Malaria== (GCS < 9)
  - ==Severe Anemia== (Hb < 5g/dL)
  - Respiratory distress / ==ARDS==
  - ==Acute Kidney Injury== (Cr > 3 mg/dL)
  - Jaundice
  - ==Hypoglycemia== (< 40 mg/dL)
  - ==Acidosis== (Lactate > 5 mmol/L)
  - Hyperparasitemia (> 10%)
  - ==Blackwater fever== (Macroscopic Hemoglobinuria)
  - Seizures
  - Circulatory collapse

## Diagnosis
- ==Peripheral Blood Smear== (Thick & Thin): Gold Standard. Species ID, parasite density. ==Giemsa stain==.
- ==Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDTs)==: Detect parasite antigens (==HRP2== for Falciparum, ==pLDH== for others/pan). Quick.
- PCR: Most sensitive. Species ID, mixed infections, resistance.

## Treatment
- Depends on Species, Severity, Resistance, Patient status.
- **Uncomplicated *P. falciparum*:** ==ACTs== (Artemisinin-based Combination Therapies) e.g., Artemether-Lumefantrine.
- **Severe Malaria (ANY species):** ==Intravenous Artesunate== (preferred). Alternatives: IV Quinine. Follow with oral ACT. Supportive care.
- **Uncomplicated *P. vivax/ovale* (non-resistant):** Chloroquine + ==Primaquine== (to clear Hypnozoites).
  - ==Primaquine== requires ==G6PD testing== --> Risk of ==Hemolysis== in deficiency.
- **Uncomplicated *P. malariae/knowlesi*:** Chloroquine.
- **Pregnancy:** 1st Trimester: Quinine + Clindamycin. 2nd/3rd Trimester: ACTs. Severe: IV Artesunate (any trimester).
- **Chemoprophylaxis:** Mefloquine, Doxycycline, Atovaquone-Proguanil.

## Prevention
- ==Vector Control==: ITNs, IRS.
- Chemoprophylaxis.
- ==RTS,S Vaccine== (specific regions/populations).
